Road Trip 9 min

Sunset Couriers

Two bike messengers race daylight to deliver apologies across a neon skyline. One carries the letter; the other carries the courage to hand it over.

Friends to maybe-more Neon city Soft action

Story

The city hummed in violet and gold as Ari tightened their helmet strap. “We’ve got forty minutes before the bridge lights cut off. Think we make it?”

“If you stop overthinking,” Sol grinned, nudging Ari’s bike with their own. They balanced a sealed envelope in their jacket—an apology letter Ari wrote but couldn’t deliver alone.

They weaved through traffic, tires singing on wet asphalt. Every red light was a tiny confession: Ari rehearsed what they’d say; Sol pretended not to hear and quietly hoped the letter was for them. Overhead, trains rattled like an impatient heartbeat.

Halfway across the river, wind whipped the envelope loose. Sol caught it with a laugh that tasted like relief. “You’re allowed to want things,” they said, tucking it back.

By the time they reached the apartment, sunset had melted into violet. Ari’s hands shook, so Sol knocked for them. When the door opened, Ari blurted everything the letter forgot: the regret, the missing, the promise to do better.

Later, coasting home under streetlamps, Sol finally asked, “Was any of that for me?” Ari’s smile cracked into something honest. “All of it is. Stay for tea?”
